Multimodal Spatial Imaging Lab

Department of Geomatics Engineering · Schulich School of Engineering · University of Calgary

Engineering Building, Room XXX

2500 University Dr NW

Calgary, AB T2N 1N4, Canada

The Multimodal Spatial Imaging Lab (MSIL) develops precision measurement methods and rigorous mathematical models for 3D reality capture using diverse imaging sensors — terrestrial laser scanners, digital cameras, range cameras, LiDAR systems, and fluoroscopic imagers.

Led by Dr. Derek Lichti, our research program sits at the intersection of imaging metrology, sensor calibration, and 3D spatial data analysis. We build the geometric and stochastic models that let sensors deliver trustworthy measurements, then apply them to problems in structural health monitoring, cultural heritage preservation, industrial quality control, and human motion capture.

Our work is grounded in the fundamentals of photogrammetry, geodesy, and estimation theory, while embracing modern computational tools — point cloud processing, optimization, and machine learning — to push the boundaries of what imaging sensors can measure.

Research Themes

  • Sensor Calibration & Modelling — Systematic error models and self-calibration methods for terrestrial laser scanners, cameras, range cameras, and mobile mapping systems.
  • Structural Deformation Monitoring — High-precision deformation measurement from point cloud surfaces using physically-motivated models.
  • Cultural Heritage Documentation — Digital preservation of at-risk heritage sites using terrestrial laser scanning and photogrammetry.
  • Biomedical Imaging Metrology — Geometric calibration of dual fluoroscopic imaging systems for human joint kinematics.
  • Industrial Metrology — Dimensional control and as-built modelling in industrial environments.

Joining the Lab

We are actively recruiting MSc and PhD students with backgrounds in geomatics engineering, surveying, computer science, or related fields. If you are interested in precision measurement, 3D sensing, or spatial data analysis, please review the research themes and contact Dr. Lichti with your CV and transcripts.
