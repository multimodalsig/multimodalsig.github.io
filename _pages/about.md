The **Multimodal Spatial Imaging Lab (MSIL)** develops precision measurement
methods and rigorous mathematical models for 3D reality capture using diverse
imaging sensors — terrestrial laser scanners, digital cameras, range cameras,
LiDAR systems, and fluoroscopic imagers.

Led by [Dr. Derek Lichti](/people/), our research program sits at the
intersection of **imaging metrology**, **sensor calibration**, and
**3D spatial data analysis**. We build the geometric and stochastic models
that let sensors deliver trustworthy measurements, then apply them to
problems in structural health monitoring, cultural heritage preservation,
industrial quality control, and human motion capture.

Our work is grounded in the fundamentals of photogrammetry, geodesy, and
estimation theory, while embracing modern computational tools — point cloud
processing, optimization, and machine learning — to push the boundaries of
what imaging sensors can measure.

### Research Themes

- **Sensor Calibration & Modelling** — Systematic error models and
  self-calibration methods for terrestrial laser scanners, cameras, range
  cameras, and mobile mapping systems.
- **Structural Deformation Monitoring** — High-precision deformation
  measurement from point cloud surfaces using physically-motivated models.
- **Cultural Heritage Documentation** — Digital preservation of at-risk
  heritage sites using terrestrial laser scanning and photogrammetry.
- **Biomedical Imaging Metrology** — Geometric calibration of dual
  fluoroscopic imaging systems for human joint kinematics.
- **Industrial Metrology** — Dimensional control and as-built modelling
  in industrial environments.
